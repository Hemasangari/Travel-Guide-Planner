Japan
  • Spring (March to May): Cherry blossom season (Sakura), Hanami (flower viewing), and numerous spring festivals.
  • Summer (June to August): Fireworks festivals (Hanabi Taikai), Obon Festival (a celebration of ancestors).
  • Autumn (September to November): Autumn foliage season, popular for hiking and sightseeing.
  • Winter (December to February): New Year's celebrations (Shogatsu), Sapporo Snow Festival.
  • Year-round: Onsen hot springs
  • Bowing is a traditional greeting, especially for formal occasions.
  • Remove shoes when entering homes, certain restaurants, and temples.
  • Japanese people value silence in public spaces like trains.
  • Japanese Yen (JPY)
  • Emergency Number: 110 (Police), 119 (Ambulance/Fire).
  • General Safety: Japan is known for being very safe, but always take care of your belongings in crowded areas.
  • Natural Disasters: Be prepared for earthquakes; familiarize yourself with evacuation procedures.
Italy
  • Spring (March to May: Easter celebrations, Siena's Palio, and the Venice Carnival.
  • Summer (June to August): Opera in Verona, coastal festivals, and art exhibitions.
  • Autumn (September to November): Grape harvest festivals, Tuscan Wine Harvest.
  • Winter (December to February): Christmas Markets, Venice Carnival.
  • Year-round: Explore historic cities
  • A handshake or kiss on both cheeks among close friends.
  • Italians dress well, so it's important to look put-together, especially in restaurants and churches.
  • Tipping is not mandatory, but rounding up the bill or leaving 5-10% is appreciated.
  • Euro (EUR)
  • Emergency Number: 112 (Police, Ambulance, Fire).
  • General Safety: Italy is generally safe but be cautious of pickpockets, especially in tourist areas like Rome or Florence.
  • Natural Disasters: Italy has some earthquake risk, especially in central and southern regions.
New Zealand
  • Spring (September to November): Wellington International Jazz Festival, and various flower festivals.
  • Summer (December to February): New Zealand International Arts Festival, outdoor events and music festivals.
  • Autumn (March to May): Autumn festivals and harvest-related events. (March-May)
  • Winter (June to August): Skiing season, Queenstown Winter Festival.
  • Year-round: Hike in stunning landscapes
  • A handshake is common, and Māori people may greet with a hongi (pressing noses).
  • Tipping is not customary but is appreciated for exceptional service.
  • Avoid littering or leaving waste behind, be mindful of your actions in nature as New Zealanders take environmental conservation seriously.
  • New Zealand Dollar (NZD)
  • Emergency Number: 111 (Police, Ambulance, Fire).
  • General Safety: New Zealand is quite safe, but always be mindful of outdoor activities and natural hazards.
  • Natural Disasters: Earthquake risk (particularly in Christchurch), and volcanic activity in some areas.
South Africa
  • Spring (September to November): Arts festivals, wine harvest celebrations.
  • Summer (December to February): New Year's Eve celebrations, Cape Town Minstrels Carnival.
  • Autumn (March to May): The Cape Town International Jazz Festival, cultural events.
  • Winter (June to August): Whale watching, especially along the coast.
  • Year-round: Visit Cape Town and Table Mountain
  • A handshake is common; in some regions, people may greet with a hug or kiss on the cheek.
  • 10-15% tipping is expected in many places.in restaurants, and tipping is appreciated for guides, drivers, and hotel staff.
  • South Africans value personal space, so avoid standing too close to others.
  • South African Rand (ZAR)
  • Emergency Number: 10111 (Police), 112 (Emergency).
  • General Safety: Exercise caution in certain urban areas and avoid walking alone at night.
  • Natural Disasters: South Africa experiences occasional floods and droughts, so stay informed about weather conditions.
New York
  • Spring: (March to May) Tribeca Film Festival, cherry blossoms in Brooklyn Botanical Garden.
  • Summer (June to August): Macy’s Fourth of July Fireworks, Shakespeare in the Park, Pride March.
  • Autumn (September to November): Halloween parades, New York Film Festival, and Thanksgiving Day Parade.
  • Winter (December to February): Holiday markets, ice skating at Rockefeller Center, New Year’s Eve celebrations.
  • Year-round: Broadway shows
  • A handshake is typical, but New Yorkers are often casual and may offer a nod or quick hello.
  • 15-20% tipping in restaurants, also for hotel staff, taxi drivers, and porters.
  • New Yorkers tend to be very direct and may not engage in small talk.
  • US Dollar (USD)
  • Emergency Number: 911 (Police, Ambulance, Fire).
  • General Safety: New York is generally safe, but be cautious in certain areas at night.
  • Natural Disasters: New York is prone to winter storms and hurricanes (particularly in coastal areas).
